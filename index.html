COMICS

Watchmen

"Watchmen" by Alan Moore and Dave Gibbons - This classic graphic novel is considered one of the greatest comic books ever written, exploring complex themes such as power, morality, and humanity through a captivating story.

Maus

"Maus" by Art Spiegelman - This Pulitzer Prize-winning graphic novel tells the story of the author's father, a Holocaust survivor, and is both a touching family memoir and a powerful commentary on the horrors of war and prejudice.

Batman

"Batman: The Dark Knight Returns" by Frank Miller - This seminal graphic novel explores an aging Batman who comes out of retirement to fight crime in a dystopian Gotham City, and is widely credited with revolutionizing the superhero genre.

Sandman

"Sandman" by Neil Gaiman, Sam Kieth, and Mike Dringenberg - This award-winning graphic novel series is a dark, imaginative journey through the world of dreams and nightmares, exploring themes of mythology, storytelling, and the human condition.

MYSTERY-THRILLER

Murder on the Orient Express

"Murder on the Orient Express" by Agatha Christie: This classic mystery follows detective Hercule Poirot as he investigates the murder of an American businessman aboard the luxurious Orient Express train.

The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes

"The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes" by Arthur Conan Doyle: This collection of short stories features the famous detective Sherlock Holmes and his sidekick Dr. John Watson as they solve a series of mysteries in Victorian England.

The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo

"The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo" by Stieg Larsson: This thriller follows investigative journalist Mikael Blomkvist as he tries to solve the disappearance of a wealthy family's daughter, with the help of the talented computer hacker Lisbeth Salander.

The Da Vinci Code

"The Da Vinci Code" by Dan Brown: This mystery-thriller follows symbologist Robert Langdon as he becomes embroiled in a dangerous conspiracy involving the Catholic Church, secret societies, and hidden messages in the works of Leonardo da Vinci.
